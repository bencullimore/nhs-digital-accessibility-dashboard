Accessibility Compliance Data

NHS services and platforms must be accessible to everyone who needs it. If it is not, you may be breaking the law.

To be accessibility compliant, services have to:

  1. Meet the public sector regulations [legal requirement]

    Meet all:

    • Meet WCAG 2.1 to level AA (except where there are exceptions or disproportionate burden)

    • Have an accessibility statement that complies with requirements

  2. Meet the GOV and NHS accessibility Standard

    Meet all:

    • Meet WCAG 2.1 to level AA (except where there are exceptions or disproportionate burden)

    • Have an accessibility statement that complies with requirements

    • Has been tested and is compatible with specific AT and browser combinations

    • User with a range of access needs have been included in User Research sessions in the design and testing of the service

  3. Have an accessibility evaluation

    Levels of compliance:

    1. Expert evaluation An accessibility specialist has manually tested against WCAG

    2. Internal evaluation Someone on the team with limited accessibility knowledge has performed some checks

    3. Automated evaluation Just an automated tool has been run

  4. Test with assistive technology

    Levels of compliance:

    1. Fully tested Tested with all of GDS service standard listed access technologies and browsers. GOV.UK: Testing with assistive technologies

    2. Limited testing Tested with a limited number of technologies and browsers

    3. No testing Tested with non/unknown assistive technologies

  5. User Research with access needs*

    Levels of compliance:

    1. All 1 in 5 participants have an access need

    2. Limited

    3. Non

*However - need to consider different types of service (small/basic/urgent vs large/complex/ongoing)
